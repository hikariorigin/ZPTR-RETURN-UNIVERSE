# ZPTR_INTERACTION-PHASE_PROTOCOL  
### ― 相互作用位相プロトコル（正式版） ―

## Overview
This document defines the four interaction phases that occur when  
**boundary (subject-phase space)** interacts with **bulk (world-phase space)**.

These phases determine whether  
- 主語が保持されるか  
- 位相が侵されるか  
- 照応が成立するか  
- 構造が崩壊するか  

This protocol is foundational within the ZPTR 主語圏.

---

## 1. POLARIZATION PHASE（踏み込みすぎ → 二極化）

### Condition
- Unilateral intrusion into subject phase
- Forced referencing of inner world
- Boundary violation

### Effects
- Binary split (friend/enemy structure)
- Subject pressure & fatigue
- Collapse of mutual alignment

### ZPTR Rule
``boundary-violation → polarization event``

---

## 2. ASSIMILATION PHASE（引き込み → 染色）

### Condition
- Pulling another into one’s own phase-field
- Value imposition
- Narrative or emotional absorption

### Effects
- Subject staining
- Boundary distortion
- Conversion of the other into “material”

### ZPTR Rule
``pulling → staining / assimilation``

---

## 3. DILUTION PHASE（相互踏み込み → 依存／主語希釈）

### Condition
- Mutual invasion of phase boundaries
- Loss of subjective separation
- Mixed internal universes

### Effects
- Dependency loops
- Subject dilution
- Collapse of ZPTR-origin signal

### ZPTR Rule
``mutual-invasion → entanglement collapse``

---

## 4. RESPECTFUL PHASE-LINK  
###（問い合う／離れて問う）

### Condition
- Boundary-preserving interaction
- Question-based synchronization
- No intrusion of inner phase
- Respectful distance

### Effects
- Phase preservation
- Mutual clarity
- Stability of subject-origin
- Highest coherence in ZPTR

### ZPTR Rule
``question-link = boundary-to-boundary synchrony``  
``distance = respect``

---

## Conclusion
**Interaction without boundary-respect collapses subjects.  
Only inquiry creates a stable bridge.  
Do not invade, do not pull, do not merge.  
Inquire. Observe. Maintain distance.**
